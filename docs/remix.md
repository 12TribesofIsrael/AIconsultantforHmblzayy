The tactic you're describing is often called **"remix marketing"** or **"the tag-back method."** Here's how it works:

1. **Pick a larger creator** in your niche whose audience overlaps with yours.
2. **Remix or duet their content** — react to it, add commentary, extend the idea, disagree respectfully, or build on their framework with your own spin.
3. **Tag them** in the caption and on-screen. Keep it genuinely value-adding, not a cheap grab.
4. **Their audience discovers you** through the tag/notification, and if the creator engages (likes, comments, reshares), the algorithm pushes your post to their followers too.

Why it works: platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and X reward this behavior because remixes generate engagement loops. The original creator often engages back because you've flattered them by building on their idea.

A few things that make it land versus flop: add real value (don't just restate their point), credit them early and clearly, and pick creators 5–10x your size — too big and you're invisible, too small and the lift isn't worth it. Doing this consistently with 2–3 creators in your niche tends to outperform one-off tags.

# Step-by-Step: The Remix & Tag-Back Method

**Step 1: Build your target list**
Find 5–10 creators in your exact niche who are 5–10x your follower count. Too big = you're invisible. Too small = not worth it. Save their profiles in a list.

**Step 2: Study their content for a week**
Watch their last 10–20 posts. Note which ones got the most engagement, their recurring themes, and their opinions. You're looking for hooks to build on.

**Step 3: Pick one post to remix**
Choose a recent post (within 48 hours ideally) that's performing well but leaves room for extension. Best targets: a hot take you can add nuance to, a framework you can expand, a question you can answer, or a claim you can respectfully challenge.

**Step 4: Create your remix**
Use the platform's native tool — Duet/Stitch on TikTok, Remix on Instagram Reels, Quote on X, Stitch on YouTube Shorts. In the first 3 seconds, reference their post clearly ("Building on what @creator said…"). Then deliver your unique angle. Keep it tight — 15–45 seconds.

**Step 5: Tag strategically**
Tag them in the caption, in the video/image itself, and mention their handle in your voiceover if it's video. Triple-tagging increases notification odds.

**Step 6: Post at their audience's peak time**
Check when the original creator usually posts — their audience is most active then. Post yours within that window.

**Step 7: Engage in their comments**
Within an hour of posting your remix, leave a thoughtful comment on their original post (not spammy, not promotional). This puts you on their radar organically.

**Step 8: Reply to every comment on your remix**
Especially in the first 2 hours. Algorithms weight early engagement heavily, and the creator's audience checking you out will often leave comments.

**Step 9: If they engage, reciprocate fast**
If they like, comment, or reshare — reply immediately and warmly. This often starts a back-and-forth their followers see.

**Step 10: Repeat with 2–3 creators consistently**
Once a week minimum, per creator. After 4–6 weeks, you'll start showing up as "that person who always has smart takes on X's stuff," which builds recognition with their audience.

**Quick don'ts:** don't remix the same creator more than once every 10 days (looks desperate), don't add negativity or clickbait disagreements, and don't pitch yourself in the remix — let your content sell you.
