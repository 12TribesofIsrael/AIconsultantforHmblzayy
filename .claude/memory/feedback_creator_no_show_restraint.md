When the creator misses a promise — no-show stream, missed live, broken commitment, anything that puts a soft moment in the room — instinct says "attention vacuum, fill it." That instinct is wrong.

**Default: stand down.** Don't post. Don't fill silence. The visible attention is *negative* attention — anything you say in that window risks highlighting the miss, capitalizing on it, or reading as virtue-signaling.

**Why:**
- Most posts that come to mind in a no-show window draw eyes back to the miss (e.g. "Where's Zay?" / "What happened?"). Bad for the creator, bad for you.
- Even a tasteful "no rush King, recovery first" post is a tightrope — it works ONLY if the room is genuinely wobbling. If it's quiet, that same post reads as virtue-signaling for clout.
- **If the creator's team posts a clarification, the window closes immediately.** The team is doing community management — that's their role. Echoing their voice ("just like the team said!") reads as piling on, not contributing. Demonstrating that you respect the team's role > demonstrating that you'll fill their gap. Especially relevant when relationship-building toward the inner circle is the goal.

**How to apply:**
- **Default action: nothing.** Don't post. Restraint reads as maturity. Showing up in every soft moment reads as eager.
- **Max action if room is wobbling AND no team voice yet:** ONE post that defends the creator without naming the miss (`"🙏 No rush King — recovery first. Family's praying and locked in for [next milestone] 🔱"`). No link. Walk away.
- **If team has spoken:** drop to react-only — single 🙏 or ❤️ on the team's message. No original post. Preserve all post equity for the next clean window.
- **What NEVER works:** anything referencing the miss, anything with a link, anything starting with "I" or "we built", "Where is X?" speculation.

**Worked example (May 1, 2026):** Zay said he'd go live on TT at 5 PM, didn't. Thomas read the no-show as opportunity. Initial guidance: tight grace post if room was wobbly, otherwise hold. Then team posted "he's resting, didn't want to go live." Window closed → emoji-react to team message only. Sunday tracker post in `#general` is still the canonical visible-post moment.
