After Thomas posts a deliverable (link, tool, answer, map, doc) into someone else's community surface — HMBL Discord, Skool, Twitch chat, etc. — the rule is **total silence on that thread until the community reacts first.**

**Why:** Thomas spelled it out on May 2 after we shipped the RV route-planning map into HMBL #general and he asked me to read the chat: *"I'm not doing anything else unless they say something else. I don't want to do anything else. Let's just let it sit, bake, and wait."* Even when the post got zero direct reactions and the conversation pivoted to other ideas (Blessed's stepfather contact, MiMi's police-escort suggestion), the right move was to NOT step on those threads with a follow-up about our drop. Re-engaging looks thirsty and farms responses; staying silent lets the post stand on its own and respects the community's pace.

**How to apply:**
- Posted a link / map / answer in a community? Walk away. Don't add "let me know what you think" follow-ups, don't tag people, don't re-share the same link with new framing.
- Even if the post gets quiet reception — **especially** if it gets quiet reception — don't double-tap. Quiet reception IS a signal (community pivoted past it, attention is elsewhere); re-posting doesn't fix it, it just reinforces "this person needs validation."
- Watch for ONE specific signal that authorizes re-engagement: someone in the community asks a substantive question that the deliverable answers. THAT is the hook to respond with substance. Until then: silent.
- Internal analysis ("here's what's happening in the chat", "here's the read") is fine and useful — but stays internal. Don't translate it into another community post.

**Distinct from existing rules:**
- `feedback_no_channel_double_tap.md` is about cross-channel outreach (waiting between DM channels)
- `feedback_creator_no_show_restraint.md` is about no-shows on creator commitments
- `feedback_community_post_deposit_first.md` is about the FIRST post needing to be a deposit
- This rule is about silence AFTER the deposit ships, even when it's our own post we're tempted to nurture
