Strip technical words ("OBS", "browser source", "stream overlay config", etc.) from any message aimed at Zay directly.

**Why:** Zay isn't the tech guy — ShuggC is. When a message to Zay contains tech jargon, his reflex is to forward it to ShuggC, which closes the loop right back to the gatekeeper and defeats the point of the direct channel. Caught live on Apr 22: an IG DM draft ended with "...real-time for your OBS" — user called it out, we stripped the OBS mention and ended with "...updates real-time. It's yours." instead.

**How to apply:** When drafting DMs, texts, or replies aimed at Zay, describe what the thing DOES in his-level terms ("live map", "shows your route + checkpoints real-time"), not the delivery mechanism. Tech terms are fine for ShuggC channel; keep Zay channel about the artifact and the outcome. Read every draft for Zay once asking "would this make him forward it to ShuggC?" — if yes, rewrite.
