When posting in **someone else's community** (HMBL University Discord, Zay's Twitch chat, anyone-not-yours), the first post on a given day or moment must be a **deposit** — pure community service with zero self-reference, zero faithwalklive.com mention, zero "I built / I made / check out my" framing.

Self-link or tracker-promo posts are **withdrawals** — only land cleanly after at least one deposit on the same surface.

**Why:**
- Order matters more than content. Even a tasteful, useful link feels opportunistic if it's your *first* contribution to the room. Same link feels generous if it's your *second*.
- During a creator's news cycle (recovery, milestone, big announcement), the room is in celebration mode — they want to support the creator, not discover what fans built. A self-link in that moment competes with the creator's moment.
- Inner-circle people (mods, team, family) read posting patterns. "Showed up to support, then later shared something useful" reads as community member. "Shared something useful, then nothing" reads as agenda.
- ShuggC routing closure on May 1, 2026 made HMBL Discord a primary surface for Thomas (no longer a fallback). Tone discipline has higher leverage now.

**How to apply:**
- **Day of creator's big news** (return announcement, milestone, etc.): drop a community-service post (e.g., "X going live in 5 — let's pull up" with NO link). That's the deposit.
- **Following day** or after the news settles: tracker post is fine, framed as service ("built this during the pause so we'd have something to keep up with"). That's the withdrawal.
- **Worked example (May 1-3 sequence):** Friday May 1 → TT live signal-boost in `#general` ("Zay going live on TikTok in 5 — let's pull up @hmblzayy 🙏"), no link. Sunday May 3 (after he walks) → tracker post, framed as service.
- **Generalizes beyond Discord** — same logic applies to first appearance in a Slack channel, Twitch chat, group DM, anywhere Thomas isn't the host.
